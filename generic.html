I am a GIS analyst focused on offshore renewable energy. I am interested in project management, multi-criteria decision analysis, and bathymetric data processing. My passion is finding new and creative methods to use these techniques to maximize renewable energy production.

I am an experienced data analyst trained in applied GIS for coastal management solutions. I hold a MSc degree in Applied Coastal and Marine Management from the University College Cork's estemed Geography Department. I graduated in 2025 with a dissertation on fixed platform offshore wind site suitability in Ireland using GIS, multi-criteria decision analysis and stakeholder engagement.

Geographic analysis is key to ensuring that offshore wind energy is successful. This is because choosing the right site to construct a wind farm is possibiliy the single most important decision in the project's development. Some of the factors that create a good offshore wind farm site are high winds, excellent electrical grid access, favorable seabed conditions, and mimimal conflicts with other water users. Selecting an optimal site involves complex geographic analysis, paired with multi-criteria decision analysis and stakeholder engagement.
